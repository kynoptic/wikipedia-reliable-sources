# Website and internal communications governance

Guidelines for HMS IT website pages, intranet content, and internal communications.

## Summary

This reference covers governance for:
- **HMS IT website pages** – Public-facing web content
- **Internal communications** – SharePoint intranet, Teams, and policies
- **Out-of-office messages** – Staff communication standards

All content follows core HMS IT style guide with platform-specific additions.

---

## HMS IT website content

### General web page guidelines

**Applies to:** All HMS IT website pages (it.hms.harvard.edu)

**Key requirements:**
- Follow core HMS IT style guide for voice, tone, and formatting
- Use sentence case in all headings and navigation
- Keep content scannable with short paragraphs and descriptive headings
- Use action-oriented language
- Ensure mobile-friendly formatting

### Page-specific guidelines

#### Strategic initiatives page
**Purpose:** Showcase major IT projects and initiatives

**Structure:**
- Brief overview of initiative
- Current status
- Expected benefits
- Timeline (if appropriate)
- Contact for more information

**Tone:** Professional but accessible, focus on value to community

#### Individual project detail pages
**Purpose:** Provide deeper information about specific projects

**Required elements:**
- Project name and description
- Problem being solved
- Solution approach
- Status and timeline
- Stakeholders and team
- Resources and links

#### Getting started page
**Purpose:** Orient new users to HMS IT services

**Characteristics:**
- Clear, sequential structure
- Essential services highlighted
- Links to detailed documentation
- Focused on new faculty/staff needs

#### Departments page
**Purpose:** Describe HMS IT organizational structure

**Format:**
- Department name and mission
- Services provided
- Contact information
- Team members (if appropriate)

#### News articles
**Purpose:** Announce updates, changes, and news

**Format:**
- Clear, descriptive headline
- Date prominently displayed
- Brief summary at top
- Detailed content below
- Related links

**Tone:** Professional, timely, relevant

### Service templates

**Standard structure for service pages:**

1. **Overview**
   - What the service does
   - Who can use it
   - Key benefits

2. **Getting started**
   - Prerequisites
   - How to access
   - Initial setup steps

3. **Common tasks**
   - Frequently performed actions
   - Step-by-step guides or links to KB articles

4. **Support and resources**
   - Related KB articles
   - Training materials
   - Support contact information

## Internal communications

### SharePoint intranet pages

**Audience:** HMS IT staff only

**Content template structure:**

1. **Page title** (sentence case)
2. **Purpose statement** (1-2 sentences)
3. **Content sections** with clear headings
4. **Related resources** (links to other pages and documents)
5. **Last updated** date
6. **Contact** for page questions

**Organization principles:**
- Group by function, not by team
- Use consistent navigation structure
- Avoid duplicate content
- Link to authoritative sources

### Microsoft Teams organization

**Channel structure:**
- **General** – Team-wide announcements and general discussion
- **Project-specific** – Dedicated channels for major initiatives
- **Topic-specific** – Organized by functional area

**Naming conventions:**
- Use sentence case
- Be descriptive and specific
- Avoid acronyms unless universally known
- Keep names concise

**Channel descriptions:**
- Brief statement of purpose
- What types of content belong there
- Related channels or resources

### Policy documentation

**Template structure:**

1. **Policy name**
2. **Purpose and scope**
   - Why policy exists
   - Who it applies to
   - When it applies
3. **Policy statement**
   - Clear, unambiguous requirements
   - Use "must" for requirements and "should" for recommendations
4. **Procedures** (if applicable)
   - Step-by-step implementation
   - Responsibilities
5. **Exceptions** (if any)
   - Conditions for exceptions
   - Approval process
6. **Related policies and resources**
7. **Effective date and review cycle**
8. **Approval and ownership**
   - Who approved
   - Who maintains

**Language:**
- Clear and direct
- Avoid legal jargon
- Use active voice
- Be specific about requirements

### Out-of-office messages

**Requirements for HMS IT staff:**

**Standard format:**
```
I am out of the office [dates] with [limited/no] access to email.

[If limited access: I will respond to urgent messages as soon as possible.]
[If no access: I will respond when I return on [date].]

For immediate assistance, contact [backup person] at [email] or [phone].
For general IT support, contact the HMS IT Service Desk at itservicedesk@hms.harvard.edu or 617-432-2000.
```

**Guidelines:**
- Include specific dates (not "this week" or "next month")
- Specify level of email access
- Provide alternative contact
- Include standard Service Desk information
- Keep professional tone
- Don't include travel details or personal information

**Examples:**

*Limited access:*
> I am out of the office December 20-24 with limited access to email. I will respond to urgent messages as soon as possible.
>
> For immediate assistance, contact Jane Doe at jane_doe@hms.harvard.edu or 617-432-1234.
> For general IT support, contact the HMS IT Service Desk at itservicedesk@hms.harvard.edu or 617-432-2000.

*No access:*
> I am out of the office December 20-31 with no access to email. I will respond when I return on January 2.
>
> For immediate assistance, contact John Smith at john_smith@hms.harvard.edu or 617-432-5678.
> For general IT support, contact the HMS IT Service Desk at itservicedesk@hms.harvard.edu or 617-432-2000.

## Communications governance documents

### Organization
**Purpose:** Meta-documentation about how HMS IT manages governance

**Principles:**
- Keep governance docs up to date
- Use consistent structure across all governance
- Store in accessible central location (SharePoint)
- Review regularly (at least annually)

### Content guidelines for governance docs
**Requirements:**
- Clear purpose statement
- Defined scope and audience
- Actionable guidance
- Examples where helpful
- Related documents linked
- Ownership and update cycle specified

## Navigation and organization

### HMS IT website
**Structure:**
- Services (primary navigation)
- Support and resources
- About HMS IT
- News and updates

**Principles:**
- User-focused organization
- Clear information architecture
- Consistent navigation patterns
- Mobile-responsive

### SharePoint intranet
**Structure:**
- By function (not by team)
- Logical grouping of related content
- Minimal clicks to information
- Clear page hierarchy

---

## Platform-specific formatting

### Web pages (HTML)
- Use semantic HTML tags
- Ensure accessibility (WCAG 2.0 AA minimum)
- Responsive design for all screen sizes
- Fast load times

### SharePoint pages
- Use built-in web parts where possible
- Maintain consistent styling
- Avoid custom CSS unless necessary
- Test in multiple browsers

### Teams

- Use built-in formatting (bold, lists, and code blocks)
- Avoid excessive formatting
- Use @mentions appropriately
- Pin important messages

## Common web/intranet issues

### Avoid these problems

**Outdated content:**

- Review pages at least annually
- Add "Last updated" dates
- Remove or archive old content
- Update links regularly

**Poor navigation:**

- Too many clicks to reach content
- Unclear page titles
- Inconsistent structure
- Broken links

**Accessibility issues:**

- Missing alt text on images
- Non-descriptive link text
- Poor color contrast
- Tables for layout (use CSS instead)

**Mobile unfriendly:**

- Text too small
- Buttons too close together
- Horizontal scrolling required
- Content cut off
