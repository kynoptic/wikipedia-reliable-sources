# Content template for HMS IT intranet pages

*Guidelines for creating and maintaining content on the HMS IT intranet to ensure consistency and alignment with user needs and departmental objectives*

## Table of contents

## Introduction

This document provides detailed guidelines for creating and managing content for the HMS IT intranet. The objective is to support content creators and managers in delivering relevant, timely, and staff-centered content that enhances internal communication and collaboration.

The guidelines apply to all content creators and managers responsible for updating the HMS IT intranet. While the primary focus is on the homepage and category pages, the principles may also extend to other sections of the intranet that require similar governance.

Effective content governance is essential for the HMS IT intranet to function as a reliable resource hub. The homepage serves as the primary entry point for staff, making it crucial that it is organized to meet their immediate needs. Similarly, category pages must be structured to provide easy access to specialized information and resources. This governance document is informed by best practices in content strategy, emphasizing usability, accessibility, and the alignment of content with organizational and staff goals. The guidelines have been developed based on feedback from IT staff and other stakeholders, ensuring that the intranet continues to support the work of HMS IT effectively.

## Summary

### Homepage

The IT intranet homepage acts as the **central hub for IT staff to access essential resources, tools, and information**, facilitating communication and collaboration across departments.

Key elements include:

1. **Featured resources** – Quick access to key tools and forms.
1. **Announcements** – Updates on important IT events and policies.
1. **Calendar** – Schedule of important IT events.
1. **Resource index** – Directory of IT tools and guides.

### Category pages

Category pages provide **centralized access to resources related to specific topics**, making it easier for staff to find and use relevant information.

Key elements include:

1. **Featured resources** – Direct links to essential resources.
1. **Additional resources** – Sub-categories for in-depth resources.
1. **Key contacts** – Updated contact info for relevant personnel.
1. **Documents** – Repository of essential forms and guidelines.
1. **Resource index** – Comprehensive directory of category-specific tools.

## HMS IT intranet content templates

### Homepage

#### Purpose

The homepage of the IT intranet serves as the primary gateway for staff to access critical resources, tools, and information. It is designed to simplify work processes, enhance internal communication, and support seamless collaboration within the IT department and across the University.

#### Content goals

- **Engage staff effectively** by providing relevant, timely, and easily accessible information.
- **Promote usage** of essential tools and resources by ensuring their visibility and ease of access.
- **Foster cross-departmental collaboration** by making essential resources and tools accessible to all staff members.

#### Audience

1. **Primary audience** – ****IT department staff.
1. **Secondary audience** – ****Staff members from other departments who require access to IT resources for cross-departmental collaboration.

#### Dependencies and considerations

- **Regular updates** – ****The homepage content, including announcements and news, must be updated to stay current and relevant.
- **Calendar maintenance** – ****The integrated calendar should be updated with IT-related events and important dates.
- **Usability and accessibility** – ****The site must be designed with user-friendly navigation and accessibility features to ensure all staff members can effectively use the site.

#### Priority guide

1. **Featured resources**
- **Intent** – ****Provide quick access to the most important and frequently used resources.
- **Content** – ****Organizational chart, request forms (for example, for training, equipment, and travel), internal procedures and processes
1. **Announcements**
- **Intent** – ****Keep staff informed about essential updates, events, and news that impact their work.
- **Content –** Highlight critical updates such as essential policy changes.
1. **Calendar**
- **Intent** – ****Help staff stay organized by providing a calendar of important events for staff.
- **Content** – Upcoming events.
1. **Resource index**
- **Intent** – ****Offer a comprehensive directory of IT resources.
- **Content** – Links to tools, platforms, and guides that support daily work and collaboration within and across departments.

### Category pages

#### Purpose

The category pages on the IT intranet provide staff with centralized access to comprehensive resources related to specific topics or areas of interest.

#### Content goals

- **Enhance resource discovery** by organizing related resources under easily navigable categories.
- **Improve efficiency** by offering quick access to each category's most frequently used and relevant resources.
- **Support knowledge sharing** by keeping key contacts and documents updated and easily accessible.

#### Audience

- **Primary audience –** IT department staff.
- **Secondary audience** – ****Staff from other departments requiring specialized IT resources or contacts.

#### Dependencies and considerations

- **Regular updates** – ****Category pages need periodic updates to keep resources and contacts current.
- **User-centered design** – The structure and layout should reflect the staff's mental models, ensuring ease of use and intuitive navigation.

#### Priority guide

1. **Featured resources**
- **Intent** – Provide direct access to each category's most essential and frequently used resources.
- **Content** – ****Quick links to essential tools, guides, and documents.
1. **Additional resources**
- **Intent** – Facilitate deeper exploration by categorizing additional resources related to the topic.
- **Content** – ****Sub-categories for more specific resources, such as advanced user guides or policy documents.
1. **Key contacts**
- **Intent** – Ensure staff can quickly find and contact the right people for category-specific inquiries or issues.
- **Content** – Up-to-date contact information for category-relevant personnel.
1. **Documents**
- **Intent** – Make essential forms, guidelines, and documents accessible to staff.
- **Content** – ****A repository of documents related to the category, with clear descriptions and downloadable formats.
1. **Resource index**
- **Intent** – ****Provide a directory of all resources within the category.
- **Content** – Comprehensive links to all tools, platforms, and documentation related to the category.
