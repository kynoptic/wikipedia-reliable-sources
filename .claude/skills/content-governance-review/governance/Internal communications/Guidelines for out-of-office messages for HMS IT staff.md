# Guidelines for out-of-office messages for HMS IT staff

*Follow these guidelines to create effective out-of-office (OOO) messages to ensure clear communication and support continuity during staff absences*

## Introduction

This document aims to provide HMS IT staff with clear and practical guidelines for writing out-of-office messages. The objectives are to ensure continuity of service, offer alternate contacts for urgent matters, and maintain a professional communication standard.

These guidelines apply to all HMS IT staff who will be out of the office for any reason. It is relevant for short- and long-term absences and covers all platforms where OOO messages might be used, such as email and team chat tools.

Effective OOO messages are critical to maintaining service levels and professional communication standards. By providing clear and concise information about absences and alternate contacts, staff can ensure that urgent matters are addressed promptly, and routine inquiries are managed efficiently.

## Summary

Maintain a professional, concise, and friendly tone. Clearly state dates for out-of-office periods and note institutional closures. Direct urgent matters to a designated contact, ensuring they have agreed to cover your duties. Provide links to self-service resources for technical assistance and always include your standardized email signature.

## Guidelines

- **Maintain professional communication** – Be concise, professional, and friendly.
- Note institutional closures – For [Harvard holidays](https://hr.harvard.edu/holiday-calendar), indicate that the School is closed and that responses may be delayed.
- **Specify exact dates** – Clearly state when you will be out of the office.
- **Refer urgent matters to a real person or monitored email** – Use a shared email address that reaches team members ready to help.
- **Confirm secondary contact availability** – Ensure the secondary contact has agreed to cover your duties.
- **Link to self-service resources** – Promote the HMS IT Service Portal and the service desk contact information for technical assistance. You can also provide links to resources like a bookings page or a request form for services related to your role.
- Include your email signature – Use the [email signature format established by Harvard Medical School](https://identityguide.hms.harvard.edu/templates).

### Examples

Follow the guidelines and maintain a professional tone but revise the suggested text to suit your voice.

#### General OOO message

> Thank you for your message.

> **I will be out of the office from Wednesday, July 3, to Sunday, July 7.** I will respond to your email when I return.

> Note that HMS is closed for the holiday on July 4 and 5.

> For urgent matters, please contact [SECONDARY_CONTACT@hms.harvard.edu](mailto:SECONDARY_CONTACT@hms.harvard.edu).

> For technical assistance, visit the [HMS IT Service Portal](https://harvardmed.service-now.com/stat)

> Best regards,

***EMAIL_SIGNATURE***

#### Complex coverage details

> Thank you for your message.

> **I will be out of the office from Monday, July 8, to Wednesday, July 10.**

> For urgent matters, please contact the following team members:

- **For scheduling and calendaring** – ***CONTACT_INFO***
- **For HR matters and IT workforce planning** – ***CONTACT_INFO***

> For technical assistance, visit the [HMS IT Service Portal](https://harvardmed.service-now.com/stat).

> Thank you for your understanding.

> ***EMAIL_SIGNATURE***

#### For individual contributors

> Thank you for your message. I'm out of the office and will return on ***DATE***.

> For urgent matters, please contact our team at [TEAM_EMAIL@hms.harvard.edu](mailto:TEAM_EMAIL@hms.harvard.edu).

> You can also find helpful resources on the HMS IT website page for ***TOPIC***.

> For technical assistance, visit the [HMS IT Service Portal](https://harvardmed.service-now.com/stat).

> Sincerely,
***EMAIL_SIGNATURE***
