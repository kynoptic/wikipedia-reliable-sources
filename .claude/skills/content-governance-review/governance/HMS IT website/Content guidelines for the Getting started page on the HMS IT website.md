# Content guidelines for the *Getting started* page on the HMS IT website—DRAFT

*Guidelines for creating user-friendly, actionable content for the Getting started page*

## Introduction

The purpose of this document is to provide comprehensive **guidelines for creating and maintaining content on the *Getting started*** **page** of the HMS IT website. It ensures that the information presented to new users is clear, consistent, and aligned with the overall goals of HMS IT. The page aims to help new users seamlessly onboard with essential IT services and security protocols while minimizing confusion and support inquiries.

This document **applies to all content published on the *Getting started*** **page of the HMS IT website**. It is intended for those who contribute to or maintain this page. The guidelines outlined here focus on the structure, messaging, tone, and calls to action (CTAs) needed to support new users, primarily faculty, staff, students, and guests requiring access to HMS IT services.

The *Getting started* page plays a critical role in the onboarding process for new HMS users, guiding them through the activation of their accounts, device connectivity, and compliance with security standards. Users often face difficulties during this initial setup phase, leading to increased support tickets and delays in adopting IT services. Clear, concise content with actionable steps is essential to mitigate these challenges.

## Summary

The page aims to help users activate accounts, connect devices, and ensure security compliance while reducing IT support tickets and promoting best practices.

The primary audience is new HMS users, including faculty, staff, and students. IT staff are the secondary audience. Key user pain points include confusion around account setup, network connection difficulties, security concerns, and uncertainty about where to find help.

## Content guidelines

### Content brief

This section is the **strategic foundation** for the *Getting started* content, detailing its overall purpose, audience, and voice.

#### Purpose and goals

- **Page objective** – This page's primary goal is to **guide new users** at HMS in setting up essential IT services and accounts and ensuring their security and connectivity.
- **User needs** – New users need a **single resource to orient them**, help them access HMS IT services, and ensure their systems comply with security standards. The content addresses everyday initial needs like account activation, device connectivity, and setting up necessary security tools.
- **Business goals**:
    - Ensure smooth onboarding for new users to reduce the number of IT support tickets.
    - Promote security best practices to protect sensitive research data and institutional assets.
    - Improve overall IT service adoption among users by providing straightforward, actionable content.

#### Audience

##### Target audience

- **Primary** – New HMS users, including faculty, staff, students, and select guests who need access to IT services.
- **Secondary** – IT staff and support representatives looking for references to guide new users.

##### Key user pain points

- Confusion about activating and setting up accounts
- Difficulty connecting devices to HMS networks
- Concerns about IT security requirements and compliance
- Uncertainty about where to find help when they encounter technical issues

#### Voice and tone

The voice and tone must be **supportive** and **informational**, balancing formality with accessibility. The content must be **clear and direct**, using plain language to reduce technical jargon, especially in instructional steps. The user should feel supported and confident in following the instructions.

#### Calls to action (CTAs)

**Primary**:

- Guide users toward activating their accounts
- Encourage users to follow instructions for securing their devices
- Provide clear links for assistance

**Secondary**: 

- Encourage users to explore more IT services
- Guide users to additional resources for technical help

### Messaging and mapping

This section outlines how specific **content fields** map to **key messages**, **CTAs**, and other strategic priorities.

#### Title

- **Content field in CMS** – `Main content > Title`
- **Purpose** – Describe the page to the user.
- **Content** – <kbd>Getting started</kbd>

#### Section headings

- **Content field in CMS** – `Components > Accordions > Accordions Section Title`
- **Purpose** – Break the content into digestible sections that map to crucial user tasks.
- **Content** – Use short, descriptive, action-oriented section headings that group content. For example:
    - <kbd>Activate your accounts</kbd>
    - <kbd>Stay secure</kbd>

#### Individual sections

##### Accordion title

- **Content field** – `Components > Accordions > Accordion Item > Accordion Title`
- **Purpose** – Help users identify the topic or task covered within.
- **Content** – Clearly and concisely labels each accordion section using just a few words. Title each section in a way that makes the content easily scannable. Titles must be action-focused or directly related to user tasks. For example:
    - <kbd>HMS account</kbd>
    - <kbd>Wireless networks</kbd>
    - <kbd>Security requirements</kbd>

##### Accordion subtitle

- **Content field** – `Components > Accordions > Accordion Item > Accordion Subtitle`
- **Purpose** – Provides a brief, one-sentence description of what the user will find in the section, offering additional context to the title.
- **Content** – The subtitle explains the user's task or the information they will gain from opening that section. Use concise, action-oriented language to describe the next steps or outcomes. For example:
    - <kbd>Activate your HMS account to access services</kbd>
    - <kbd>Choose from these wireless options to connect to the internet</kbd>
    - <kbd>Help protect the integrity of our education and research missions</kbd>

##### Accordion labels

- **Content field** – `Components > Accordions > Accordion Item > Accordion Label 1` & `Accordion Label 2`
- **Purpose** – Assist users in quickly identifying the types of tasks or topics the section addresses.
- **Content** – Labels must be concise, one- or two-word descriptions that describe the content for easy scanning. For example:
    - <kbd>Activation</kbd>, <kbd>Documentation</kbd>
    - <kbd>Wi-Fi</kbd>, <kbd>Registration</kbd>
    - <kbd>Apps</kbd>, <kbd>Supported OS</kbd>

##### Accordion content

- **Content field** – `Components > Accordions > Accordion Item > Accordion Content`
- **Purpose** – Core instructional or descriptive content for the user.
- **Content** – Main body text that explains the steps, instructions, or details related to the task or topic in the section. Break the information down into short paragraphs or bullet points to maximize readability. Provide clear, actionable steps supplemented by external resources or links when necessary. For example:
    - <kbd>To activate your HMS account, follow these simple steps…</kbd>
    - <kbd>Choose from these wireless options to connect to the internet…</kbd>
