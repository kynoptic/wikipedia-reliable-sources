# Content guidelines for the Strategic initiatives page—DRAFT

*Guidelines for creating and maintaining content on the* Strategic initiatives *page of the HMS IT website*

### Introduction

**The *Strategic initiatives*** **page highlights IT strategy** by combining big-picture messaging with practical, actionable details that support user needs and demonstrate alignment with the HMS mission.

**Users need to understand the purpose and benefits of major IT initiatives** and the projects that support them. For projects that directly affect them, they also need practical details, such as timelines and required actions.

**The organization needs to communicate the transformative contributions of HMS IT**, increase visibility into the strategic portfolio, and provide a centralized, reliable source of information for major initiatives.

### Summary

- **Page structure** – The page consists of an introduction and a series of accordions, where each accordion represents a major strategic initiative.
- **Introduction** – Use aspirational yet practical language to frame IT initiatives as key drivers of the HMS mission.
- **Initiative accordions** – Present each strategic initiative as a collapsible accordion. The accordion title is the name of the initiative. Inside, provide a detailed summary of the initiative, with featured projects that support it presented with a clear, descriptive title and a benefit-oriented description.

### Component guidelines

*This section breaks down the content type into its core components and provides specific rules for each.*

#### Page title

- **CMS field** – Title
- **HTML tag** – `<h1>`
- **Purpose** – To clearly identify the page’s purpose to showcase major IT initiatives.
- **Guidance**:
    - Use *Strategic initiatives* as the title.

#### Introduction

- **CMS field** – Body
- **HTML tag** – `<p>`
- **Purpose** – To provide a strategic overview that frames the role of IT initiatives in advancing the HMS mission.
- **Guidance**:
    - Begin with a brief, aspirational statement about how IT initiatives advance the School’s mission in education, research, and administration.
    - Explain that the page describes current initiatives and the projects that support them.
- **Example**:
- At HMS IT, strategic initiatives are central to advancing our mission in education and research, as well as supporting efficient administration.
- This page describes current initiatives and related projects transforming our digital environment. These efforts, led by HMS IT in partnership with the community, are designed to enhance collaboration, improve infrastructure, and provide the tools our faculty, students, and staff need to succeed.

### Featured initiatives

- **CMS field** – Body
- **HTML tag** – `<h2>`
- **Purpose** – To label the main content section featuring the initiatives and their projects.
- **Guidance** – Use the text *Featured initiatives*.

#### Initiative accordion item

- **CMS field** – Accordion.
- **HTML tag** – `<h2>` for accordion title
- **Purpose** – To present a single strategic initiative and its associated projects in a collapsible, organized format.
- **Guidance**:
    - **Accordion title** – The accordion's title must be the name of the strategic initiative (for example, *Enhancing research*).
    - **Accordion subtitle** – The subtitle describes the initiative and summarizes the content in just a few words.
    - **Accordion tags** – Accordion tags act as signposts for users browsing the page, providing one to two-word text that indicates the accordion’s content.
    - **Accordion content** – The content within the accordion must be structured as follows:
        1. **Initiative summary** – Begin with a paragraph summarizing the initiative’s goals and purpose.
        1. **Projects** – Mention key projects supporting this initiative. Provide a brief, **benefit-oriented description** of projects if needed. Link to more information (if available), formatted as bold text – **Learn more about [Project]**.
- **Example**:
- Enhancing research

> *Supporting the responsible storage of sensitive data to unlock new funding streams*

> <kbd>Compliance</kbd>, <kbd>Data lifecycle</kbd>

- Meeting the needs of HMS researchers by improving high-performance computing, application availability, storage services, and regulated data environments. We also support the responsible use of AI through specialized environments with advanced access and integration options.
    - **Research data storage optimization** – Strengthening the sustainability, accessibility, and security of HMS research storage by expanding data cleanup activities and introducing sustainable data lifecycle practices. This work ensures shared resources remain available for discovery and supports a culture of responsible data stewardship.
        - **Learn more about research data storage optimization.**
    - **NIST 800-171 compliant environment (LASER)** – Enabling researchers to work with controlled unclassified information (CUI) by implementing a secure data environment that meets federal compliance requirements, thereby unlocking new research funding opportunities.
